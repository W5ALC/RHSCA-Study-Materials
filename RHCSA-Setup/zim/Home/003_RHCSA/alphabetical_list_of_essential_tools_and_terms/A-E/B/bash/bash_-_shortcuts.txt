**Summary:**
This article dicusses useful shortcuts in the bash shell.

**bash - shortcuts:**
* **cursor controls (added these to Anki 9/25/18 => bash shortcuts):**
	* ALT+B: GOTO previous word in current line.
	* ALT+F: GOTO next word in current line.
	* CTRL+A: GOTO beginning of current line.
	* CTRL+B: GOTO previous character in current line.
	* CTRL+E: GOTO end of current line.
	* CTRL+F: GOTO next character in current line.
	* CTRL+XX: Switch between beginning of line and current cursor position.
* **history controls (TO BE COMPLETED LATER):**
	* ALT+R:
	* CTRL+G:
	* CTRL+J:
	* CTRL+P:
	* CTRL+R:
* **manipulating text (added these to Anki 9/25/18 => manipulating text in bash):**
	* ALT+.: Print last word of most recently issued command.
	* ALT+C: Capitalize character that cursor is currently on and then go to end of current word.
	* ALT+D: Delete character that cursor is currently on.
	* ALT+L: Make all characters in current word lowercase from current cursor position forward.
	* ALT+T: Swap last 2 words before cursor.
	* ALT+U: Make all characters in current word uppercase from current cursor position forward.
	* CTRL+K:
		* Cut line from current position to end of line.  Add to clipboard.
		* If at beginning of line, cut the entire line.
	* CTRL+T: Swap last 2 characters before cursor.
	* CTRL+U:
		* Cut line from current position to beginning of line.  Add to clipboard.
		* If at end of line, cut the entire line.
	* CTRL+W: Cut word before cursor and add to clipboard.
	* CTRL+Y: Paste most recently cut item in clipboard to current line.
* **opening a terminal:**
	* CTRL+ALT+T
	* SUPER_KEY+T
